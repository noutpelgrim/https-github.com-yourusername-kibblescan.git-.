Menadione (Vitamin K3)

PROTOCOL VIOLATION Restricted Agent

Clinical Definition

A synthetic naphthoquinone derivative used as a source of Vitamin K activity. Unlike natural Vitamin K1 (Phylloquinone) found in plants, Menadione is chemically synthesized coal tar derivative. It is banned in human supplements due to toxicity risks but permitted in animal feed.

Toxicological Mechanism

  • Oxidative Stress: Induces cytotoxicity via reactive oxygen species (ROS) generation.
  • Hepatic Strain: Requires extensive liver metabolism to become bioavailable.
  • Bio-Accumulation: Linked to hemolytic anemia in high concentrations.

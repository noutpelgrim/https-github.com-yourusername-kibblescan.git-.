Yellow #5 (Tartrazine)

UNNECESSARY Class: Synthetic Azo Dye

Compound Definition

Tartrazine is a synthetic lemon-yellow azo dye primarily used as a food coloring. It is derived from coal tar and is one of the most widely used artificial food dyes.

Industrial Function

Used strictly for cosmetic appeal to the human purchaser. Dogs have limited color vision and do not select food based on color. The inclusion of Yellow #5 serves no nutritional or palatability function for the animal.

Toxicology and Risk Profile

Risk Vector: Hypersensitivity & Neurotoxicity

Tartrazine has been linked to allergic reactions (asthma, urticaria) in sensitive individuals. More recent studies suggest a link between azo dyes and behavioral neurotoxicity (hyperactivity) in models, though canine-specific data is limited.

Key Citations:
  • Lockey, S.D. (1959). Allergic reactions due to F D and C Yellow No. 5. Annals of Allergy.
  • McCann, D. et al. (2007). Food additives and hyperactive behaviour... The Lancet.

Registry Guidance

While not acutely toxic like BHA, Yellow #5 represents an "Unnecessary Risk." It adds non-zero risk for zero nutritional benefit. It is a marker of low-quality formulation.

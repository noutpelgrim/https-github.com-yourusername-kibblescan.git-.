CAS: 7758-98-7 | INORGANIC SALT

Copper Sulfate

PROTOCOL VIOLATION Inferior Bioavailability

Clinical Definition

An inorganic compound used as a copper supplement. While copper is essential, the sulfate form acts as a pro-oxidant in storage and has significantly lower bioavailability than chelated copper (Copper Proteinate).

Risk Factors

  • Hepatic Accumulation: High risk for copper-storage disease prone breeds (Bedlington Terriers, Labs).
  • Nutrient Destruction: Accelerates oxidation of Vitamin E in the bag.
  • Standard: Should be replaced by Chelated Copper in premium formulations.

Registry Status

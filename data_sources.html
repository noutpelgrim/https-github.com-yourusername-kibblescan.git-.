Data Sources & Limitations

This document outlines the acquisition vectors, classification standards, and known limitations of the KibbleScan Registry. This platform acts as independent public infrastructure, not a guarantee of safety.

1. Data Acquisition Vectors

The Registry aggregates data from three primary streams. We do not accept direct unverified feeds from manufacturers without independent sampling.

  • Optical Character Recognition (OCR): User-submitted scans of physical packaging labels (Guaranteed Analysis, Ingredient Decks) in retail environments.
  • Regulatory Filings: Publicly available recall notices from FDA CVM (Center for Veterinary Medicine) and international equivalents.
  • Independent Assay: Third-party laboratory analysis reports submitted by "Guardian" tier users (verified via Chain of Custody).

2. Classification Standards

Ingredient definitions and toxicity thresholds are derived from the following established bodies. KibbleScan does not inventing new toxicology, but rather indexes existing constraints.

  • AAFCO (Association of American Feed Control Officials): Official Ingredient Definitions and Nutrient Profiles.
  • NRC (National Research Council): Nutrient Requirements of Dogs and Cats (2006).
  • Peer-Reviewed Toxicology: PubMed-indexed studies regarding specific agents (e.g., Menadione cytotoxicity, BHA carcinogenicity).

3. Exclusionary Criteria

We explicitly EXCLUDE the following data sources to maintain neutrality:

  • Marketing claims found on manufacturer websites (e.g., "Premium," "Holistic," "Human Grade" without validation).
  • Paid placement or "Featured Product" requests.
  • Unverified API feeds that contradict physical label scans.

4. Known Limitations

Users must be aware of the inherent gaps in the current surveillance capabilities:

Group Definitions
Labeling laws permit vague terms like "Meat Meal" or "Natural Flavor." We cannot chemically identify the specific source species of these ingredients via OCR alone.
Formula Drift / Lag Time
Manufacturers may update recipes silently. The Registry reflects the last confirmed scan, which may vary from the bag currently on the shelf.
Contaminant Screening
Standard scans read the declared label. They do not detect undeclared contaminants (Mycotoxins, Pentobarbital) unless a specific Lab Assay is attached to the record.
Veterinary Disclaimer:
KibbleScan is an information aggregator, not a diagnostic tool. Classifications (e.g., "Protocol Violation") refer to the KibbleScan Internal Standard, not federal law. Always consult a licensed veterinarian regarding your animal's specific dietary needs and medical conditions.
